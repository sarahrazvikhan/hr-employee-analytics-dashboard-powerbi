# HR's Employee Analytics Dashboard

## Overview
This project is an **HR Analytics Dashboard** built in **Power BI** to analyze employee attrition and workforce trends.  
The dashboard helps identify patterns across age, salary, education, job roles, and years at the company.

It is designed to give quick business insights through KPI cards, charts, and summary tables.


## Project Highlights
This Power BI project covers:

- Data Cleaning and Processing in Power BI
- Power BI Dashboard Setup
- Import Data in Power BI
- Power Query in Power BI
- DAX in Power BI
- Measures and Calculations in Power BI
- Charts in Power BI
- Filters and Slicers in Power BI
- Dashboard Design in Power BI
- Exporting and Sharing Power BI Dashboard
- Extracting Insights from Dashboard

## Dashboard Sections

### 1. Department Filter
Users can filter the dashboard by department:

- Human Resources
- Research & Development
- Sales


### 2. Attrition by Age and Gender
A comparison view showing attrition split by:

- Male
- Female

This helps understand which gender has higher attrition in different age-related groups.


### 3. Attrition by Education
A donut chart showing attrition distribution across education backgrounds such as:

- Life Sciences
- Medical
- Marketing
- Technical Degree

This helps identify which education groups are more affected by attrition.


### 4. Attrition by Age Group
A column chart showing attrition across age ranges:

- 18–25
- 26–35
- 36–45
- 46–55
- 55+

This helps reveal which age bracket has the highest employee loss.

### 5. Attrition by Job Role
A bar chart showing attrition count by role, including:

- Laboratory Technician
- Sales Executive
- Research Scientist
- Sales Representative

This highlights the job roles with the highest turnover.

### 6. Attrition by Salary Slab
A horizontal bar chart showing attrition based on salary categories:

- Upto 5k
- 5k–10k
- 10k–15k
- 15k+

This helps analyze whether lower or higher salary bands are linked to attrition.


### 7. Attrition by Years at Company
A line chart showing employee attrition based on tenure at the company.

This helps identify at which stage employees are most likely to leave.


### 8. Job Role Matrix
A matrix table displaying attrition count by job role across rating or category columns.

This allows a more detailed breakdown of attrition distribution across roles.

## Key Insights
Some major insights from the dashboard:

- Employees in the **26–35 age group** show the highest attrition.
- **Laboratory Technicians** and **Sales Executives** have the highest employee turnover.
- Employees in the **lower salary slab** are more likely to leave.
- Certain education backgrounds, such as **Life Sciences** and **Medical**, contribute more to attrition.
- Attrition appears higher during specific early-to-mid tenure periods.
